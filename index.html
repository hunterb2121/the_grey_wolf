Basic Information

Description

The wolf is the largest Canidae. It is also differentiated by the broad snout, short ears, short torso, and longer tail. It is slender and very powerful. It's legs are also longer than other typical canids. Wolves have a large head, strong jaw, and blunt snout. The teeth are very good for crushing bone. Females are typically a little smaller than males. Wolves have a wide varity of coats with some being thinner and some being thicker depending on the environment they live in. They also come in many different colors also depending on the environment and habitat the species is typically from.

Diet

The wolf feeds mainly on wild herbivorous hoofed mammels like deer, moose, and elk. In North America, the diet consists of large hoofed mammels and medium sized mammels, including elk, moose, caribou, white-tailed deer and mule deer. In Europe and Asia, the diet consists of medium hoofed mammels and domesticed species, including moose, red deer, roe deer, and wild boar. Wolves will also hunt smaller animals whwen needed as well. The have also been known to eat certain fruit and berries as well as grass.

Habitat

Wolves are from Eurasia and North America. Their habitat has been reduced by two-thirds because of human predation. Wolves can live in many climates depending on their subspecies. They range from the arid Middle East, to the Cascade Locks, to the Arctic. They prefer wilderness and remote areas. They are typically foudn between sea level and 3,000 meters. Their habitat depends on many different factors from prey, to humans, to water, to snow.
