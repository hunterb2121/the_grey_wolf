Subspecies of Canis Lupus

Eurasian Subspecies

Tundra wolf

A large, light-furred species in the Northen Tundras and Forests of Russia

Arabian wolf

A small species adapted for a more arid environment. It's coat becomes long in the winter and short in the summer. It inhabits parts of the Middle East.

Steppe wolf

A species of average size with short, coarse hair. It is native to Northen Ukraine, Southern Kazakhstan, the Caucasus and the Trans-Caucasus.

Himalayan wolf

The Himalayan Wolf has a sharp, long face with an elevated brow, large ears, and an earthy brown coat. They are found in the Himalayas and Tibeten Platau above 4,000 meters.

Mongolian wolf

The Mongolian Wolf is a species that has a coat with a long ridge that has black and grey hairs intermixed. They are also almost all white on their underside with an grey-brown coat.

Dingo and New Guinea singing dog

They are generally 52-60cm tall, 117-125cm long, and 13-20kg on average. They have reddish-brown fur. They inhabit Australia and New Guinea.

Domestic dog

Dogs have a lot of subspecies because they are distantly related to most modern wolves by an ancient ancestor. Ancient humans domesticed dogs to help them with tasks like hunting and searching. Humans still use dogs today for certain tasks, but we also keep them as companions.

Indian wolf

The Indian Wolf is large species with light gray fur. It ranges through a lot of Eurasia and is the most prominant subspecies.

North American Subspecies

Arctic wolf

The Arctic Wolf is medium-sized with an almost completely white coat of fur. They live on Melville Island and Ellesmere Island.

Mexican wolf

The Mexican Wolf is the smallest wolf in North America with dark fur. They are found in Southwestern New Mexico, Southeastern Arizona, and Northern Mexico

British Columbian wolf

The British Colombian Wolf is a small wolf with dark fur that eats mainly fish and small deer. They live in coastal British Columbia and the coastal Yukon.

Vancouver Island wolf

The Vancouver Island Wolf is a medium sized wolf with grayish fur. It lives on Vancouver Island, BC.

Hudson Bay wolf

The Hudson Bay Wolf is a small, light-colored wolf that inhabits the Northern Manitoba and Northwest Territories in Canada.

Northern Rocky Mountain wolf

The Northern Rocky Mountain Wolf is medium to large in size with pale fur. It inhabits the northern Rocky Mountains.

Labrador wolf

The Labrador Wolf is a medium, light-colored wolf that inhabits the Northern Quebec and was recently found in Newfoundland.

Alexander Archipelago wolf

The Alexander Archipelago Wolf a medium, dark-colored wolf that inhabit the Alexander Archipelago in Alaska.

Eastern wolf

There are two forms of Eastern Wolf: a small, reddish-brown one named the Algonquin Wolf; and a larger, grayish-brown one naemd the Great Lakes Wolf. The Algonquin form occupies central Ontario and southwestern Quebec, particularly in and nearby protected areas, such as Algonquin Provincial Park in Ontario, and possibly extreme northeastern U.S. and western New Brunswick. The Great Lakes form occupies northern Ontario, Wisconsin and Minnesota, the Upper Peninsula of Michigan and southern Manitoba. Overlaps of the two forms occur, with intermixing in the southern portions of northern Ontario.

Mackenzie River wolf

The Mackenzie River Wolf has variable fur and varies in size between small and large. It lives in the Northwest Territories in Canada.

Baffin Island wolf

The Baffin Island Wolf is the smallest species of wolf in the arctic and has white, fluffy fur. It lives on Baffin Island.

Northwestern wolf

The Northwestern Wolf is very large and typically light-colored. It lives in Alaska, the Yukon, the Northwest Territories, BC, Alberta, Saskatchewan, and the northwest USA.

Greenland wolf

The Greenland Wolf is small to medium with light fur. It lives in Greenland and the Queen Elizabeth Islands.

Alaskan Interior wolf

The Alaskan Interior Wolf has a large skull with large teeth. They have black fur, white fur, or a mixture.

Red wolf

The Red Wolf has a brownish or cinnamon colored coat. It is average size and has average features from other subspecies. It lives in North Carolina after being driven out of its other territory in other southern states.

Alaskan tundra wolf

The Alaskan tundra Wolf is a large, white-colored wolf. It lives in the Barren Grounds of the Arctic Coast.

Extinct Subspecies

Kenai Peninsula wolf

The Kenai Peninsula Wolf was one of the largest species of wolf on North America. We don't know it's fur color. It lived in the Kenai Peninsula, Alaska.

Newfoundland wolf

The Newfoundland Wolf was medium and white-colored. It's old territory of Newfoundland is being retaken by the Labrador Wolf which is a relative species.

Banks Island wolf

The Banks Island Wolf was large and slender with a narrow muzzle. It lived on Banks and Victorian Islands in the Canadian Arctic.

Florida black wolf

The Florida Black Wolf was jet black that was similar in size to the Red Wolf. It lived in Florida before going extinct in 1908.

Cascade Mountains wolf

The Cascade Mountains Wolf was dark cinnamon in color and lived in the Cascade Range.

Mississippi Valley wolf

The Mississippi Valley Wolf was medium but slender with a tawny coat with a mixture or cinnamon, black, white, and grey. It lived in and around the lower Mississippi River Basin before going extinct in the 1980s. They have been reintroduced to public outcry due to the larger and more predatory nature of the Mississippi Valley Wolf compared to the native wolf of the area, the Timber Wolf.

Manitoba wolf

The Manitoba Wolf is large and grey-white. They lived in Northern Alberta, Saskatchewan, and Manitoba.

Hokkaidō wolf

The Hokkaidō Wolf was a range of sizes between the common North American wolves. It lived in Hokkaido, Sakhalin, the Kamchatkan Peninsula, and Iturup and Kunashir Islands just to the east of Hokkaido in the Kuril Archipelago.

Japanese wolf

The Japanese Wolf was a small wolf. It lived on the Japanese islands of Honshū, Shikoku, and Kyūshū (but not Hokkaido).

Mogollon Mountains wolf

The Mogollon Mountains Wolf was a small, dark-colored wolf. It lived in Arizona and New Mexico.

Texas wolf

The Texas Wolf was similar in size to the Mogollon Wolf and is possibly the same species. It lived in Texas, New Mexico, and northern Mexico.

Great Plains wolf

The Great Plains Wolf was medium and light-colored. It lived throughout the Great Plains from southern Manitoba and Saskatchewan southward to northern Texas.

Southern Rocky Mountain wolf

The Southern Rocky Mountain Wolf was medium and light-colored resembling the Great Plains Wolf, though larger with more blackish hairs on the black. It lived in Southeastern Idaho, southwestern Wyoming, northeastern Nevada, Utah, western and central Colorado, northwestern Arizona, and northwestern New Mexico.

Sicilian wolf

The Sicilian Wolf was a very slender wolf with short legs, light and tawny fur, and around the same size as the Arabian Wolf and Japanese Wolf. It lived on the island of Sicily.
